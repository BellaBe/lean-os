# Bias Selection Matrices

Quick-reference tables for selecting optimal biases based on context.

---

## By Goal

### Getting Attention (Opens, Clicks, Views)

| Priority | Bias | Implementation |
|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | Distinctiveness | Break category conventions in subject/headline |
| 2 | Curiosity (Info Gap) | Hint at secret/insight without revealing |
| 3 | Pattern Interrupt | Open with unexpected statement |
| Support | Loss Framing | Frame as "what you're missing" |

### Creating Interest (Engagement, Time on Page)

| Priority | Bias | Implementation |
|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | Social Proof | Peer companies, specific numbers |
| 2 | Authority | Credible sources, expert backing |
| 3 | Reciprocity | Give valuable insight/content first |
| Support | Framing | Concrete over abstract |

### Driving Action (Conversion, Response)

| Priority | Bias | Implementation |
|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | Scarcity | Real time/quantity limits |
| 2 | Loss Aversion | "Stop losing X" framing |
| 3 | Friction Reduction | Single CTA, minimal fields |
| Support | Present Bias | "Start today" / defer cost |

### Closing Deals (Purchase, Signature)

| Priority | Bias | Implementation |
|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | Commitment | Reference prior statements |
| 2 | Anchoring | Value before price |
| 3 | Social Proof | Peer buyers, competitor usage |
| Support | Scarcity | Deadline if legitimate |

### Retaining/Expanding (Renewals, Upsells)

| Priority | Bias | Implementation |
|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | Sunk Cost | "You've invested X time/data" |
| 2 | Commitment | "Given your goals..." |
| 3 | Reciprocity | Surprise value before ask |
| Support | Loss Aversion | "Don't lose access to..." |

### Getting Referrals

| Priority | Bias | Implementation |
|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | Reciprocity | After exceptional value delivered |
| 2 | Social Proof | "Many customers like you share..." |
| 3 | Commitment | "You mentioned loving the product..." |

## By Persona

### Executive / C-Suite

**Effective:**
| Bias | Why | How |
|------|-----|-----|
| Authority | Respect expertise | Cite credible sources, board-level proof |
| Scarcity | Time is money | Get to point fast, clear deadlines |
| Social Proof | Peer validation | Other executives, board companies |
| Loss Framing | Risk awareness | Competitive threats, market shifts |

**Less Effective:**
- Detailed arguments (too long)
- Technical depth (delegate to teams)
- Curiosity hooks (prefer direct)

**Format:** Short, outcome-focused, authority signals prominent

### Technical / Engineering

**Effective:**
| Bias | Why | How |
|------|-----|-----|
| Proof/Evidence | Skeptical mindset | Data, studies, benchmarks |
| Specificity | Detail-oriented | Concrete numbers, specs |
| Operational Transparency | Want to understand how | Explain mechanism |
| Logic | Rational processing | Clear cause-effect |

**Less Effective:**
- Pure social proof without data
- Emotional appeals
- Vague claims

**Format:** Detailed, evidence-based, logical flow

### Founder / Entrepreneur

**Effective:**
| Bias | Why | How |
|------|-----|-----|
| Distinctiveness | Value innovation | Novel approaches, contrarian takes |
| Peer Social Proof | Founder network | Other founders, YC companies |
| Vision/Future Pacing | Think big | Long-term impact |
| Reciprocity | Network-minded | Valuable intros, insights |

**Less Effective:**
- Corporate speak
- Excessive process
- Herd behavior framing

**Format:** Direct, vision-oriented, peer references

### Risk-Averse / Corporate

**Effective:**
| Bias | Why | How |
|------|-----|-----|
| Loss Aversion | Avoid mistakes | "Don't risk X" |
| Social Proof | Safety in numbers | Many similar companies use |
| Guarantees | Risk mitigation | Money-back, SLAs |
| Authority | Trust established | Certifications, enterprise customers |

**Less Effective:**
- Aspirational/gain framing
- "Be first" messaging
- Innovation positioning

**Format:** Thorough, risk-addressed, proven

### Innovative / Early Adopter

**Effective:**
| Bias | Why | How |
|------|-----|-----|
| Distinctiveness | Want to be different | Novel, contrarian |
| Exclusivity | Early access appeal | Beta, invite-only |
| Future Pacing | Ahead of curve | "Before everyone else" |
| Curiosity | Intellectually curious | Surprising insights |

**Less Effective:**
- Herd behavior ("everyone uses")
- Safety messaging
- Conventional approaches

**Format:** Novel, exclusive, forward-thinking

## By Stage

### Cold / Unaware

| Bias | Role | Implementation |
|------|------|----------------|
| Distinctiveness | Get noticed | Break pattern in outreach |
| Curiosity | Create interest | Hint at insight |
| Reciprocity | Earn attention | Lead with value |

**Avoid:** Scarcity (no relationship), commitment (no prior yeses)

### Aware / Interested

| Bias | Role | Implementation |
|------|------|----------------|
| Social Proof | Validate interest | Peer companies, numbers |
| Authority | Build credibility | Sources, expertise |
| Reciprocity | Deepen relationship | More value |

**Avoid:** Aggressive urgency (too early)

### Evaluating / Considering

| Bias | Role | Implementation |
|------|------|----------------|
| Anchoring | Set value frame | Value before price |
| Social Proof | Validate choice | Case studies, testimonials |
| Commitment | Build yeses | "Does this make sense?" |
| Framing | Optimize perception | Loss vs gain, concrete |

### Deciding / Purchasing

| Bias | Role | Implementation |
|------|------|----------------|
| Scarcity | Create urgency | Real deadlines |
| Commitment | Leverage prior yeses | "You mentioned..." |
| Loss Aversion | Motivate action | Cost of inaction |
| Choice Architecture | Guide to optimal | Decoy, defaults |

### Post-Purchase / Customer

| Bias | Role | Implementation |
|------|------|----------------|
| Commitment | Reinforce decision | "Great choice" |
| Peak-End | Ensure good memory | Strong onboarding end |
| Reciprocity | Build expansion | Surprise value |

## By Challenge

### Quick Reference Table

| If struggling with... | Primary Biases | Quick Implementation |
|----------------------|----------------|----------------------|
| Low email open rates | Curiosity, Distinctiveness | New subject line format |
| Low email replies | Reciprocity, Micro-commitment | Give first, ask question not meeting |
| Low click-through rate | Zeigarnik, Single CTA, Urgency | Open loops, remove competing links |
| Low landing page conversion | Friction, Loss Frame | Remove fields, reframe headline |
| Cart abandonment | Present Bias, Loss Aversion | BNPL option, "cart expires" messaging |
| Price objections | Anchoring, Temporal Reframe | Value before price, per-day cost |
| Customers choose cheapest | Decoy, Compromise, Loss Frame | Make low tier limited, show what's excluded |
| Difficulty raising prices | Present Bias, Loss Frame | Announce early, grandfather existing |
| "Why you vs competitor?" | Distinctiveness, Social Proof | What makes you different + peer proof |
| Low trust / new brand | Messenger Effect, Operational Transparency | Credible endorsers, show how it works |
| Skepticism about claims | Concrete Language, Messenger | Third-party validation, explain mechanism |
| Long sales cycles | Commitment, Scarcity | Build yeses, legitimate deadlines |
| High churn | Sunk Cost, Peak-End | Remind of investment, better endings |
| Low demo-to-close | Commitment, Anchoring | More yeses in demo, value before proposal |
| Onboarding drop-off | Illusion of Progress, Friction | Pre-fill steps, show 2/5 complete |
| Forgettable brand | Von Restorff, Keats, Mere Exposure | Distinctive assets, rhyming tagline |
| No referrals | Reciprocity, Timing | After success moment, after value delivery |

### Detailed Challenge Breakdowns

#### Cart Abandonment

**Symptoms:**
- Users add to cart but don't checkout
- Drop-off at payment step
- Price shock at total

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Present Bias | BNPL option, installment framing | +15-30% checkout completion |
| Loss Aversion | "Your cart expires in 24h" | +5-15% recovery |
| Temporal Reframing | Show price per day/week | +10-20% value perception |

**Secondary:** Scarcity ("Only 2 left"), Sunk Cost (remind of browsing time)

**Diagnostic questions:**
1. Where exactly do users drop off?
2. Is total price a surprise?
3. How long is checkout flow?
4. Is there a BNPL option?

---

#### Low Click-Through Rate

**Symptoms:**
- Opens but no clicks
- Engagement dies mid-content
- CTA ignored

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Zeigarnik Effect | Open loops that close at CTA | +15-30% CTR |
| Single CTA | Remove competing links | +20-40% click concentration |
| Urgency | Time-sensitive hook near CTA | +10-20% immediate action |

**Secondary:** P.S. line with scarcity, social proof near CTA

---

#### Customers Choose Cheapest Option

**Symptoms:**
- Most users select lowest tier
- Premium features unused
- Low LTV

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Decoy Effect | Make lowest tier obviously limited | +52pp shift possible |
| Compromise Effect | Position target in middle | +20-30% middle selection |
| Loss Aversion | Show what lower tiers DON'T include | +15-25% upgrade |

**Secondary:** "Most Popular" badge on target tier, limit seats on premium

---

#### Difficulty Raising Prices

**Symptoms:**
- Customer pushback on increases
- Churn risk from price changes
- Competitor price pressure

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Present Bias | Announce far in advance (pain feels smaller) | Reduced backlash |
| Loss Aversion | Frame as "locked in" for existing customers | Retention |
| Illusion of Effort | Justify with improvements/investments | Acceptance |

**Price increase playbook:**
1. Announce 3-6 months early
2. Grandfather existing customers (loss aversion)
3. Explain investment/effort behind improvements
4. Offer annual lock-in at current rate

---

#### Low Trust / New Brand

**Symptoms:**
- Users need multiple touchpoints
- High research behavior
- "Never heard of you" objection

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Messenger Effect | Partner with credible voices | +3x persuasion |
| Social Proof (Specific) | Exact numbers, named customers | +14% vs vague |
| Operational Transparency | Show how it works, behind-the-scenes | +20% satisfaction |

**Trust-building sequence:**
1. B2B endorsements (Oatly barista strategy)
2. Specific social proof (numbers, names)
3. Operational transparency (how it works)
4. Risk reversal (guarantee, trial)

---

#### Skepticism About Claims

**Symptoms:**
- "Sounds too good to be true"
- Requests for proof
- Comparison shopping

**Claim credibility checklist:**
- Every claim should be either:
  1. Specific and measurable ("15 minutes saves 15%")
  2. Third-party validated (study, testimonial)
  3. Mechanistically explained (how/why it works)

**Primary Biases:** Concrete Language, Messenger Effect, Operational Transparency

---

#### Onboarding Drop-off

**Symptoms:**
- Drop-off after signup
- Features unused
- Time-to-value too long

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Illusion of Progress | Pre-fill first steps, show 2/5 complete | +19% faster completion |
| Friction Reduction | Minimize required steps, defer optional | +conversion per step removed |
| Implementation Intentions | "When will you complete X?" prompt | +39pp follow-through |

**Onboarding checklist:**
1. How many steps required before value?
2. Is progress shown?
3. Can steps be pre-filled?
4. Is there a specific "when" commitment?

---

#### Forgettable Brand / Low Recall

**Symptoms:**
- Low unaided awareness
- Generic perception
- Ads don't stick

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Von Restorff Effect | Distinctive assets (color, shape, character) | +3x recall |
| Keats Heuristic | Rhyming tagline | +17% truth, +3.5x memory |
| Mere Exposure | Consistent distinctive execution | Preference over time |

**Memory audit:**
1. What's visually distinctive?
2. Is there a sonic/verbal hook?
3. What category conventions are you following (stop)?

---

#### Low Landing Page Conversion

**Symptoms:**
- Traffic but no signups
- High bounce rate
- Users leave at CTA

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Friction Reduction | Reduce form fields, single CTA, social login | +20-40% per friction point removed |
| Loss Aversion | Frame around what they lose by not acting | +15-25% vs gain frame |
| Social Proof (Implied) | Show usage indicators, real-time signups | +10-20% trust |

**Secondary:** Scarcity (if offer-based), Concrete Language (audit abstract claims)

**Diagnostic questions:**
1. How many clicks to convert?
2. How many form fields?
3. Is the CTA above fold?
4. Is value proposition concrete or abstract?

**Priority order:** Friction → Concrete → Social → Urgency → Loss → Anchor

#### Low Email Open Rates

**Symptoms:**
- <20% open rate
- Declining engagement
- Unsubscribes increasing

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Information Gap | Subject lines that hint but don't reveal | +20-40% open rate |
| Concrete Language | Specific numbers/outcomes in subject | +10-15% open rate |
| Loss Aversion | "You're about to lose..." subject lines | +15-25% open rate |

**Secondary:** Keats Heuristic (test rhyming), Personalization (name + context)

**Subject line formulas to test:**
1. "[Number] [things] that [outcome]"
2. "The [thing] I shouldn't share..."
3. "You're losing [X] every [period]"
4. "Why [common belief] is wrong"

---

#### Price Objections / Sticker Shock

**Symptoms:**
- Users engage but bounce at pricing
- "Too expensive" feedback
- Competitors win on price

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Decoy Effect | Add dominated option to make target attractive | +30-50% target selection |
| Temporal Reframing | $X/day instead of $X/year | +40-50% value perception |
| Illusion of Effort | Communicate iterations, expertise, time | +20-90% value perception |

**Secondary:** Anchoring (show higher first), Value stacking (list everything), Nostalgia (heritage if applicable)

**Price presentation checklist:**
1. Is there a decoy option?
2. Is price shown per day/week/month?
3. Is there an anchor (higher price first)?
4. Is effort/process communicated?
5. Is value stacked (everything included)?

---

#### Low Repeat Usage / Retention

**Symptoms:**
- Users try once and leave
- Low DAU/MAU ratio
- Feature adoption stalls

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Variable Reward Schedule | Randomize rewards, notifications | +2x engagement vs predictable |
| Sunk Cost | Surface investment ("You've created X") | Retention boost |
| Illusion of Progress | Show achievement, streaks, levels | +19% completion |

**Secondary:** Near-Win Effect ("Almost there" mechanics), Regret Lottery (auto-enter, require action to win)

**Retention diagnostic:**
1. What's the aha moment? How fast do users reach it?
2. What investment have they made? Is it visible?
3. Are rewards predictable or variable?
4. What's the re-engagement trigger?

---

#### Commodity Market / Me-Too Products

**Symptoms:**
- Price competition
- Features easily copied
- "Why you vs competitor?"

**Primary Bias Stack:**

| Bias | Implementation | Expected Impact |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Von Restorff Effect | Category-breaking distinctiveness | +3x recall |
| Illusion of Effort | Unique process/origin story | Value differentiation |
| Messenger Effect | Unique credibility angle | Trust differentiation |

**Distinctiveness audit:**
1. List 10 category conventions
2. Which can you break meaningfully?
3. What's your "Liquid Death" angle?
4. What can competitors NOT say?

**Differentiation angles:**
- Process (how you make it)
- Origin (where it comes from)
- Expertise (who's behind it)
- Audience (who it's specifically for)
- Philosophy (what you believe)

## Bias Compatibility Quick Check

**Layer well together:**
- Loss + Scarcity (loss of opportunity)
- Social Proof + Concrete (believable proof)
- Effort + Premium Price (justified)
- Curiosity + Zeigarnik (compound hooks)
- Commitment + Consistency (ladder of yeses)

**Conflict / Use carefully:**
- Scarcity + Trust building (false scarcity kills trust)
- Heavy discount + Premium (mixed signals)
- Multiple CTAs + Conversion (decision paralysis)
- Loss framing + Positive brand (can feel threatening)

**Max per piece:** 4-5 biases. More feels manipulative.
